Werkbundsiedlung

The Werkbundsiedlung were building exhibitions organised by the Deutscher Werkbund - as well as its Czech, Swiss and Austrian counterparts - between 1920 and 1930. They usually involved the construction of new neighbourhoods or colonies (siedlung) on the outskirts of the cities where the exhibition was organised, where various architects, both German and from other countries, built single-family houses designed by them, generally in the rationalist style.

Events and Dates

  • 1927 Werkbundsiedlung Stuttgart (Weißenhofsiedlung)
  • 1928 Werkbundsiedlung Brno (Nový Dům)
  • 1929 Werkbundsiedlung Breslau (WUWA)
  • 1929 Werkbundsiedlung Karlsruhe (Dammerstocksiedlung)
  • 1932 Werkbundsiedlung Viena
  • 1932 Werkbundsiedlung Neubühl (Zürich-Wollishofen)
  • 1932 / 33 Werkbundsiedlung Praga (Baba)
